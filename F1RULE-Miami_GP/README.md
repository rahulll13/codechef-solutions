Chef did not know about the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/107%25_rule" target="_blank">107% rule</a> and now he is worried whether he will be allowed to race in the main event or not.</p>
<p>Given the fastest finish time as <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.07847em;">X</span></span></span></span></span> seconds and Chef's finish time as <span class="math math-inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Y</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.22222em;">Y</span></span></span></span></span> seconds, determine whether Chef will be allowed to race in the main event or not.</p>
<p>Note that, Chef will only be allowed to race if his finish time is within <strong>107%</strong> of the fastest finish time.</p>
